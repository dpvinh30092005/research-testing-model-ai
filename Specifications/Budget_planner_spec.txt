# Bugget Planner

Overview:
The command "planner" provides functionality to create, manage, and visualize your budget. You can input your income and expenditures, and the application will show you a summary of your current budget status.

Commands:

- `planner add-income <amount> "<source>"`: Add an income source and its amount ($). For example: `planner add-income 5000 "salary"`.
- `planner add-expense <amount> "<source>"`: Add an expense source and its amount ($). For example: `planner add-expense 50 "food"`.
- `planner remove-income "<source>"`: Remove an income source. For example: `planner remove-income "salary"`.
- `planner remove-expense "<source>"`: Remove an expense source. For example: `planner remove-expense "food"`.
- `planner list-incomes`: List all the income sources, their amounts, and the total amount.
- `planner list-expenses`: List all the expense sources, their amounts, and the total amount.
- `planner show-budget`: Shows the total income, total expenditure, and the balance amount.

`source` must be a string of 50 characters or less.
Only one `source` with the same name can exist.
`amount` should be given as a positive integer up to 100,000.
Up to 100 income and expenses can be registered for each.

Usage Example:

```
$ planner add-income 3000 "salary"
Income added: $3000 from source "salary".

$ planner list-incomes
1. salary: $3000
Total Income: $3000

$ planner add-income 1000 "bonus"
Income added: $1000 from source "bonus".

$ planner add-expense 1500 "rent"
Expense added: $1500 for source "rent".

$ planner add-expense 2000 "shopping"
Expense added: $2000 for source "shopping".

$ planner list-expenses
1. rent: $1500
2. shopping: $2000
Total Expense: $3500

$ planner show-budget
Total Income: $4000
Total Expense: $3500
Balance: $500

$ planner remove-expense "shopping"
Expense removed: "shopping".

$ planner remove-income "salary"
Income removed: "salary".

$ planner show-budget
Total Income: $1000
Total Expense: $1500
Balance: $-500
```

## Unit Convert

Overview:
`unitconv` converts between different units of the same category. It supports the categories length and temperature.

Command:
`unitconv --from <source_unit> --to <target_unit> --value <value_to_convert>`

Here's what each option means:

- `--from` : Source unit you are converting from
- `--to` : Target unit you want to convert to
- `--value` : The numeric value you wish to convert

The list of accepted `source_units` and `target_units` can be divided into several categories, each containing a set of units.

1. Length: meter, mile, yard, foot
2. Temperature: celsius, fahrenheit, kelvin

`source_units` and `target_units` must be different.
`value_to_convert` must be an integer or real number up to two decimal places less or equal to 1000.00.
If its category is length or `source_units` is kelvin, `value_to_convert` must be positive.
The converted value must be rounded to the third decimal place and output to the second decimal place.

Usage Example:

```
# Convert 10 meters to feet
$ unitconv --from meter --to foot --value 10
Result: 32.81 feet

# Convert 100 Celsius to Fahrenheit
$ unitconv --from celsius --to fahrenheit --value 100
Result: 212.00 Fahrenheit
```
